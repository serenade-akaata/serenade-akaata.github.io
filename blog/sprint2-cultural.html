Learning Plan

Long Term Goals

My long term goal has always been to pursue a career in web developing, where I would build websites, create apps and along the way learn a thing or two about game developing. Although I’m quite open to the large range of roles in the tech industry, I’ve always wanted to work for a big company, whether it be in web development or gaming. Coding is a passion for me and I guess I’ve always wanted to make my parents proud. (yes I’m still about that mind-set and love it).

Skills

While at EDA, I want to be comfortable in working in a close environment and to be able to speak my mind (at appropriate times of course) to ensure my learning. In related to my personality test I took, I’m a very introvert person (95%) to be exact, so being in a space which encourages teamwork, will definitely help me gain the skills needed to take to a future job in tech hopefully.

Strengths and weaknesses

Family and friends tend to identify me as Honest and determined. When I put my mind to something I know is worth my time I will do everything in my power to make sure I’m successful in whatever the task or activity is. When it comes to Honesty, people tend to either take it the wrong way or misunderstand what I’m saying. I truly do suck at explaining what is in my mind but I will never lie to someone and if there’s something I truly believe in, I will let my opinions be heard.

As for weaknesses I tend to keep to myself a lot. But then again who’s to say that’s a bad thing. I’m an introvert what can I say? I will literally remove myself from equations if it involves any type of interaction with people I don’t really know and in this industry that’s not a weakness I should have. So I’m still working towards this.

Managing Workload

I tend to get stuck in rabbit holes a lot and if I’m being honest it’s the only reason why I lose track of time. I’m not complaining that it isn’t fun problem solving but sometimes it is draining. If we’re talking about how much time I have a week that goes towards this course, it is a lot. I’ve made a plan that I try to follow every day, which tells me when to take a break between each study period.

I will be following my own planner I drew up specifically for foundations and will use the suggested time box given for each task, that way I won’t be spending unnecessary time. If however I do manage to get stuck, I will need to evaluate what I’m not understanding or why I’m still on a task. Then unstuck myself and move on.

Seeking Help

When seeking help I tend to make sure that I’ve broken down what I’m being asked first before anything. If that still doesn’t work, I’ll try researching and exploring the web for a abit before asking others in my cohort if they came across similar barriers or if they could help. Seeking help from my facilitator is my last resort, not saying that asking for help is a bad thing, but me being able to figure something out on my own is sort of an accomplishment for me. But there will always be times that I will seek help from someone with more knowledge than I have on something.

Facilitation Team Expectations

My expectations for the Facilitation team are:

  • Explaing tasks that need a clearer understanding
  • Be available to answer any queries
  • Giving helpful suggestions
  • Reviewing your work and giving feedback on what’s good and things to work on or research.
