Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

Prior to really going deep and thinking about what Identity, values and strengths meant to me, I decided to take the Myers Briggs test. Although the results were not surprising at all, I ended up doing more research on what each attribute meant and found out I’m a perfectionist (which can be a good and bad personality trait to have). My personality type is Mediator INFP – T. In other words I scored high in being an Introvert, being Intuitive, following my feelings when making decisions and emotions, being prospecting when approaching work, planning and decision-making, and lastly having a turbulent personality.

Values

After pondering on situations where I’ve had to make an ethical decision, my mind went completely blank. Although one example of an ethical decision I had to make was when I was a lot younger than I am now. I grew up in a large family, me being the youngest and only girl with five brothers, my parents were always strict on me doing chores and my brothers always bossed me around. Fast forward a couple of years and I got abit slack on doing things, I’d leave it to the next day to do or I just wouldn’t do it at all. (I know lol) Anyways my dad thought it was a good idea to start paying us, thinking it would encourage us to do more things. It wasn’t until then that I really realised how important it was to them. Obviously I didn’t take the money. Reasons because 1, their family, which is one of my major personal values and 2, it just wasn’t right.

I grew up in a catholic church and my beliefs since I was little were and are always going to be the same. Culture is also a huge influence in the reasons why I do things. Being a pacific islander, there are always assumptions of how we are portrayed. The way I see things is, if I were to take that money from my parents I would be taking from myself and knowing how hard it was for my parents to come to a completely new country and earning that money to raise a family is what influences me in my everyday decisions.

In terms of my personal values some major ones in my life would be honesty, family and determination, and that’s just a few. Honesty is so big to me. Bottom line is I hate people who lie. Always have and always will, no matter the circumstances. I think honesty to me means that someone is willing to trust you with something whether it be personal or hard to explain and know that you will try to understand them. Family is an easy one for me. Family is family (whether your blood or not) you can always rely on each other and know you can count on them to have your back.

Stengths

In terms of strengths I think I can list the ones I think I have, that are already self-explanatory, and that way I won’t need to go to deep in explanation.

  • Honest
  • Determined
  • Reliable
  • Loyal

Weaknesses

With strengths there comes weaknesses and there are always limitations when it comes to my time management. I’m normally good with getting things done on time, but there are the occasional times where I will get distracted. Of course there are times where I actually do have a good reason for not doing something just like now (Getting an extension because the flu in my house is like a spreading virus). But I acknowledge that my time management hasn’t always been good and im working on that.

Working experience with others

I’m a former University student and I learnt that, there’s nothing wrong with that. I left because I felt that what I was studying wasn’t where I was using my knowledge at its full potential. In my last year attending, I joined a cultural group and was elected as treasurer. For those who may not know what that is, I handled the money and the accounts associated with that association. So I was always making statements and chasing people up on things. Along with a treasurer, there were many other roles filled by other uni students and of course there was bound to be some conflict. There were always problems with people missing meetings, not paying fees and just not showing up. We discussed what the problems were and eventually came to the solution of communication. Communication is vital when working with others and that’s something I can take away from this experience and put it towards future situations.
